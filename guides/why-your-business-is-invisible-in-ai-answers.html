7 Reasons Your Business is Invisible in AI Answers (and How to Fix Each)

AI is changing how people find businesses. If your business isn't showing up in AI answers, you're missing out. Here's why that might be happening and what you can do.

  1. Your Website Lacks Clear, Specific Information. AI models learn from the text on your website. If you use vague language or industry jargon, AI might not understand what you offer. Be direct. Clearly state your products, services, location, and hours. Use simple terms that anyone can understand.
  1. You Aren't Using Relevant Keywords. AI searches for keywords that match user queries. If your website doesn't use the terms people are searching for, AI won't find you. Research common search terms for your industry and location. Integrate these naturally into your website's content, headings, and descriptions.
  1. Your Business Isn't Listed on Key Directories. AI often pulls information from online directories like Google Business Profile, Yelp, and industry-specific listings. If your business isn't listed or your information is inconsistent across these platforms, AI will struggle to verify and present your details.
  1. Your Online Presence is Inconsistent. AI prioritizes accurate and consistent information. If your business name, address, or phone number (NAP) is different on various online platforms, AI may flag it as unreliable. Ensure your NAP details are identical everywhere your business is listed online.
  1. You Don't Have Enough Local Signals. For local businesses, AI looks for local relevance. This includes local citations, reviews from local customers, and mentions on local websites or news outlets. Building a strong local presence signals to AI that you are a relevant business for local searches.
  1. Your Website Isn't Mobile Friendly. Many AI searches happen on mobile devices. If your website is difficult to navigate or slow to load on a phone, AI may deprioritize it. Ensure your website is responsive and provides a good user experience on all devices.
  1. You Haven't Focused on Structured Data. Structured data, or schema markup, is code that helps search engines and AI understand the content on your page. Properly implemented schema for your business type, products, services, and location can make it much easier for AI to extract and display your information accurately.
