Here's the part owners miss: a caller with an emergency will not wait. If you don't pick up, they dial the next name on Google within a minute. An auto text-back that fires in under 60 seconds keeps a chunk of those jobs yours.

Your 2-minute teardown — how to plug the leak

  1. Catch the miss instantly. The moment a call goes unanswered, fire an automated text back to that number — not a voicemail, a text. Most people read a text in minutes; almost nobody calls back a voicemail.
  2. Answer in under 60 seconds. Speed-to-lead is the whole game. The first to respond usually wins — so the text has to be automatic, not "when I get a free minute."
  3. Make the first text book, not greet. A booking ask beats "we'll call you back" — offer a time and a next step in the very first message.
  4. Measure recovered vs. cost. Track contacts recovered, appointments booked, and dollars in. If one recovered job covers months of tooling, the math is no longer a guess.
